## WHY MISPRICING PERSISTS (Economic Analysis)

### 1. Transaction Costs (0.15-0.25%)
- Brokerage: 0.02-0.05% each leg
- Exchange fees: 0.01%
- Bid-ask spread: 0.05-0.10%
- **Total**: ~0.15-0.25%

**Impact**:
- For Rs. 1,000 contract: Rs. 1.50-2.50 cost
- Mispricing of 0.30% = Rs. 3 opportunity
- After costs: Rs. 0.50-1.50 profit (50% margins)

### 2. Financing Constraints
- Cash-and-carry requires borrowing spot capital at 6-7% p.a.
- For 11-month position: ~5.5-6.4% cost = Rs. 57-68 per Rs. 1,000 investment
- Mispricing of 0.3% only generates Rs. 3 profit
- **Cannot justify capital deployment for 11 months to earn 0.3% when risk-free rate is 6.2%**

### 3. Margin Requirements
- Futures: 30% initial margin
- Spot: 100% capital required
- **Simultaneous position**: Needs Rs. 58.5L for Rs. 45L arbitrage
- Most traders lack this capital availability

### 4. Execution Risk
- Cannot execute spot + futures simultaneously
- While buying spot: Futures may move away
- Liquidity impact: Large orders move prices (0.5-1% slippage)
- Technology latency: 100-500ms between legs
- **Window for adverse price movement**: Very tight margins

### 5. Market Microstructure
- Bid-ask spreads widen during thin trading
- Volatile days: Spreads expand, arbitrage opportunities disappear
- Small traders: Last to execute, capturing worst prices
- Fast traders (HFTs): Capture arbitrage before others
